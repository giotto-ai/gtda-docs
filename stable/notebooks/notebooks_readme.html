Can two-dimensional topological voids exist in two dimensions?¶

The classic example of a two-dimensional homology class is the “void” surrounded by a sphere in three-dimensional space. Challenge question: Can two-dimensional topological voids arise from point clouds in two-dimensional space? We will answer this question programmatically by computing Vietoris-Rips persistence homology of random point clouds in the square \([0, 1] \times [0, 1] \subset \mathbb{R}^2\).
